### Abstract (English)

The Patagonian king crab (*Lithodes santolla*) fishery in Tierra del Fuego represents an ideal case study for implementing data science technologies in Argentine fisheries management. Despite holding MSC certification, operating a small and controllable fleet, and building upon a solid biological research base developed by CADIC and INIDEP, a significant gap exists between globally available advanced Deep Learning and Bayesian Network methodologies and their current application in managing this fishery. This article synthesizes the state of the art in artificial intelligence applications for fisheries, identifies specific gaps for crustaceans, and proposes a methodological framework based on Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) as an innovative tool for decision-making under uncertainty. The DL_Bayesian project is presented as a proof of concept integrating Bayesian networks, causal analysis, and deep learning into a complete MLOps system, demonstrating the technical feasibility of these approaches for the regional context. Data used at this stage are synthetic, calibrated from scientific literature; validation with real data constitutes Phase 1 of the proposed R&D+i agenda (2026‚Äì2027).

### Descripci√≥n del proyecto (campo de texto libre)

This working paper presents an interdisciplinary research agenda at the intersection of deep learning, Bayesian inference, and fisheries science. The core contribution is identifying that **Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) have never been applied in fisheries management globally**, representing a significant research gap.

**Key findings from the literature review:**

1. Deep Learning applications for crustaceans are notably underdeveloped compared to finfish
2. No LSTM or Transformer model has been published for king crab stock prediction
3. BNNs, which combine deep learning's predictive power with Bayesian uncertainty quantification, are virtually absent from fisheries literature
4. Argentina's technological gap in AI for fisheries presents both challenges and first-mover opportunities

**The DL_Bayesian proof of concept demonstrates:**

- Bayesian Network structure learning for causal modeling of fishery variables
- BNN implementation with variational inference for prediction with uncertainty intervals
- Causal intervention analysis using do-calculus for counterfactual scenario evaluation
- A complete MLOps architecture (PyTorch + pgmpy + FastAPI + MLflow + Docker)

**Important methodological note:** Current implementation uses synthetic data calibrated from published values for Patagonian crustacean fisheries. Real-data validation with INIDEP survey data and CADIC historical records is planned as Phase 1 (2026‚Äì2027) of the proposed R&D+i agenda.
